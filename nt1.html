Nam Theun 1 Power Company Limited (NT1PC)

Project Over view

NT1PC was authorized by the Government of the Lao People's Democratic Republic (GOL) to develop, own and operate the Nam Theun 1 Hydropower Project (NT1HPP).

The company has entered into a concession agreement (CA) with the GOL since 14 June, 2016, the concession period of which spans 27 years.

Project Owner:

NT1PC is jointly owned by three shareholders: Phonesackgroup Company Limited (PSG), Electricity Generating Public Company Limited (EGCO), and EDL-Generation Public Company (EDL-GEN).

Project Location:

The Project is located on the Nam Kading River in Bolikhamxay Province, approximately 33 km upstream of its confluence with the Mekong, and 220 km from Vientiane Capital, Laos. The Nam Theun River is one of the major tributaries of the Mekong River in Lao PDR, with a catchment area of 13,856 km2 at the dam site. The Nam Theun is formed by serveral tributaries draining south-westwards from the Annamites to the Nakai Plateau. The NT1 Project is the last power project in the Nam Theun-Nam kading Hydropower Cascade.

Project Agreement:

On 23rd September, 2017, NT1PC signed and enterd into the Power Purchase Agreement with the Electricity Generating Autority of Thailand (EGAT) and the Electricite du Laos (EDL) - these agreements are also referred to as the EGAT PPA and the EDL PPA respectively. The total installed capacity, with 3 generating units under 2 PPAs, is 650 MW, of which 2x260 MW will be supplied to EGAT and 130 MW to EDL for the duration of 27 years commercing from the Commercial Operation Date (COD) scheduled in May 2022.
